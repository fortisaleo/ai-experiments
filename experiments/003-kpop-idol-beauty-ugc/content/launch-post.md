Most people are looking at AI UGC as a way to make cheaper creator videos.

I think that misses the bigger shift.

AI UGC is starting to look like synthetic media franchises:

- reusable characters
- repeatable product worlds
- song hooks
- visual systems
- batch generation pipelines

So I built one.

Experiment 003 is LUMINA, a fictional five-member K-pop beauty group made with fal.ai.

The product is fictional too: Lumora glow serum.

The campaign is five 15-second solo beauty ads, one for each member:

- Rae: premium vanity glow
- Jia: chrome girl-crush stage hook
- Sena: soft vocalist glow ritual
- Nari: dance-performance pre-stage momentum
- Yumi: approachable backstage bloom

The stack:

1. MiniMax Music 2.6 for the LUMINA song hook
2. GPT Image 2 for fictional idol casting
3. GPT Image 2 edit for member reference frames
4. Seedance 2.0 reference-to-video for the final clips
5. ffmpeg for final video export

The important workflow choice:

I did not start with a talking-head script.

I started with the song.

For this format, the song defines the world:

- pearl pink
- chrome
- backstage vanity
- performance lights
- dewy pre-stage glow
- Lumora as the remembered word

Then every visual prompt has something to orbit.

The casting pass used archetypes, not lookalikes:

- luxury beauty muse
- girl-crush performer
- elegant solo vocalist
- dance ace
- fresh approachable idol

No real idol names.
No celebrity likeness.
No logos.
No clinical skincare claims.

The first casting pass was strong enough that I kept all five candidates as the group.

That changed the experiment from "make one AI UGC idol" into "build a reusable synthetic campaign system."

The batch completed with five finished solo videos:

- Rae
- Jia
- Sena
- Nari
- Yumi

The production layer was not perfectly clean.

Reference-frame success:

- Rae: 3/5
- Jia: 1/5
- Sena: 3/5
- Nari: 1/5
- Yumi: 5/5

But all five final Seedance videos still succeeded.

That is the practical lesson.

A real operator workflow cannot depend on every intermediate generation being perfect. It has to preserve the source portrait, use successful references, record failures, and still finish the batch.

The original faceless UGC thread was the inspiration.

This does not prove the income claims.

It tests the production question:

Can one operator build a fictional cast, song, product world, visual language, and five reusable solo ads through a fal.ai workflow?

For this experiment, yes.

With caveats.
